Case Study

We have worked on a redesign of the current Huff Rise website in order to deliver this experience in the best way possible.

Effectively Identifying Our Objectives

We've thought extensively about how users interact with video and the best way to present this online. We have worked on a redesign of the current Huff Rise website in order to deliver this experience in the best way possible.

  • Mobile First

    We thought starting with mobile was the most important decision in order to create a consistent flow for users on all devices as many users will be most likely to use this medium for viewing video.

  • Video Behaviour

    One of the main design elements that was implemented into desktop was that of the scrolling video behaviour in order for users to fully engage with the video they are watching.

  • Content Discovery

    A category menu was implemented into a hamburger within the new design in order for users to easily choose from and discover new content by just scrolling.

Current Issues With HuffPost Rise

Below are the goals that we considered to be the most important for the redesign and an enabler for shaping every design decision we made during the process.

  • Navigation causing clutter on homepage
  • Main content was no divided up in uniform which disturbed the flow
  • Newsletter section lacked affordance as it appeared like an advertisement
  • Columned content gave users impression that all videos were advertisements
  • Page loader slowed down navigation between pages
  • Footer contained most important navigation for the website
  • Hamburger navigation contained less important information for the user

Our Solution to Improve User Experience

We broke down the current design and tried to understand the importance of each section in the eyes of users. A new flow was created for the site in order to maximise time spent on the website and inform users effectively.

Designing Rise in a Responsive Way

Starting with mobile gave us to opportunity to map out a flow that users can experience in a consistant way. In doing this we kept the design fluid from mobile to tablet and finally to desktop.
